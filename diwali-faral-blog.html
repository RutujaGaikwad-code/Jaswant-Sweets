Celebrate Diwali with Jaswant Sweets' Faral Collection

Traditional Maharashtrian snacks crafted with generations of culinary wisdom

January 20, 2024

Diwali in Maharashtra is synonymous with the tradition of Faral - an exquisite assortment of sweet and savory snacks that bring families together. At Jaswant Sweets, we've perfected these traditional recipes over three generations, offering Kolhapur's most authentic Diwali Faral collection.

"The crackle of fresh Chakli, the delicate sweetness of Shankarpali, the aromatic filling of Karanji - these are the flavors that make a Maharashtrian Diwali truly special."

Our Signature Faral Selection

Golden Chakli

Our spiral-shaped savory delights are made from a perfect blend of rice flour, gram flour and aromatic spices. Each batch is fried in pure ghee to achieve that signature golden hue and satisfying crunch.

Specialty: Enhanced with roasted cumin and sesame seeds for extra flavor

Diamond Shankarpali

These diamond-cut sweet morsels strike the perfect balance between crispy and melt-in-mouth. Made with premium wheat flour, pure ghee and topped with delicate sugar crystals.

Specialty: Our secret recipe ensures they stay fresh for weeks

Crescent Karanji

Half-moon shaped pockets of crisp pastry filled with sweetened coconut, nuts and cardamom. Each piece is hand-crimped by our experienced chefs for that perfect traditional look.

Specialty: Filling made with freshly grated coconut and jaggery

Festive Ladoo Varieties

From besan to motichoor, our Diwali special ladoos are prepared with extra care using premium ingredients. Each ladoo is hand-rolled to perfection.

Specialty: Made with aromatic spices and pure ghee

Pro Tip for Faral Storage

"Store your faral in airtight containers with a piece of blotting paper at the bottom to absorb any excess oil. Keep in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight to maintain freshness throughout the festive season."

The Cultural Significance of Faral

In Maharashtrian culture, Diwali faral represents:

Family Bonding

The preparation and sharing of faral brings generations together, creating cherished memories

Community Sharing

Exchanging faral with neighbors and friends strengthens community ties during Diwali
