Celebrate Ganesh Chaturthi with Jaswant Sweets' Divine Modak

Discover our authentic Ukadiche Modak, Lord Ganesha's favorite sweet, made with traditional recipes passed down through generations

August 25, 2024

At Jaswant Sweets, the preparation of Modak for Ganesh Chaturthi is not just a culinary process - it's a sacred ritual. Our Ukadiche Modak (steamed Modak) carries the blessings of three generations of sweet makers who have perfected this divine offering to Lord Ganesha.

"Modak is not just a sweet - it's prasadam. Every fold in the Modak's pleats carries our devotion, and every ingredient is selected with the same care we would use for offerings in a temple." - Late Shri Jaswant Rao, Founder

The Significance of Modak in Ganesh Chaturthi

According to Hindu mythology, Modak is Lord Ganesha's favorite sweet. The shape of Modak symbolizes the universe, with the outer covering representing the material world and the sweet filling representing spiritual bliss. At Jaswant Sweets, we honor this tradition by:

  • Preparing Modak only after morning prayers during Ganesh Chaturthi
  • Using only the freshest ingredients sourced from trusted local suppliers
  • Maintaining strict hygiene standards as befits a divine offering
  • Preparing small batches throughout the day to ensure freshness

Our Traditional Modak Recipe

What makes our Ukadiche Modak special is the perfect balance between the soft, delicate outer covering and the rich, flavorful filling:

Key Ingredients

For the Outer Covering:

  • Freshly stone-ground rice flour
  • Pure ghee
  • Rock salt
  • Water from Tuljapur's holy well

For the Filling:

  • Freshly grated coconut from Konkan
  • Organic jaggery from local sugarcane
  • Cardamom powder from Kerala
  • Nutmeg (just a pinch for aroma)

The Art of Modak Making

Creating the perfect Modak requires skill passed down through generations. Here's how we make ours:

Preparing the Dough

The rice flour is carefully roasted in ghee to remove raw flavor, then mixed with hot water to form a smooth, pliable dough. The temperature of the water is crucial - too hot and the dough becomes sticky, too cool and it cracks when shaped.

Making the Filling

Fresh coconut is grated and cooked with jaggery until the mixture thickens. We add cardamom and nutmeg at just the right moment to preserve their delicate aromas. The filling must be moist but not runny.

Shaping the Modak

This is where years of practice show. A portion of dough is shaped into a cup, the filling is added, and then the edges are carefully pleated to form the characteristic Modak shape with exactly 21 folds - considered auspicious.

Steaming to Perfection

The Modaks are steamed for exactly 12 minutes in muslin-lined steamers. This gentle cooking preserves the delicate texture while ensuring the dough is cooked through.

Types of Modak We Offer

While Ukadiche Modak is traditional, we offer several varieties to suit different tastes:

Traditional Ukadiche Modak

The classic steamed version with coconut-jaggery filling, just as Lord Ganesha prefers it.

Fried Modak

A crispier version with the same delicious filling, perfect for those who enjoy texture contrast.

Chocolate Modak

A modern twist with cocoa-infused outer layer and chocolate-coconut filling, popular with children.

Why Choose Jaswant Sweets' Modak?

In Kolhapur, many sweet shops offer Modak during Ganesh Chaturthi, but here's why ours stand out:

  • Authentic Taste: We don't cut corners - our recipes remain unchanged for generations
  • Freshness Guarantee: We make Modak in small batches throughout the day
  • Artisan Craftsmanship: Each Modak is shaped by hand by our experienced chefs
  • Purity: No artificial colors, flavors, or preservatives - just as tradition dictates

Serving and Storage Tips

To enjoy our Modak at their best:

  • Serve Ukadiche Modak warm for the best texture and aroma
  • Fried Modak can be served at room temperature
  • Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days
  • Reheat steamed Modak by steaming for 2-3 minutes (not in microwave)
  • Best consumed within 24 hours of purchase for optimal taste
